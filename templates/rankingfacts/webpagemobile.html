Making rankings transparent and interpretable
Ranked lists are ubiquitous: We rank job applicants, search results, grant proposals, or construction bids, to prioritize them based on some notion of quality. A ranking can be the result of a seemingly simple process - compute the score of each item using a formula and then sort items on the score - or of a complex machine learning method. Whether the ranking methodology is simple or complex, its result can be difficult to interpret. Ranking Facts uses a “nutritional label” metaphor to present a standardized, human-interpretable summary of the ranking methodology and of its result.
